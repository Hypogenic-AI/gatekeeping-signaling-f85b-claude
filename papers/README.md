# Downloaded Papers

45 PDFs covering theory of signaling/screening, empirical studies of journal/conference gatekeeping, peer-review noise and bias, and the modern AI-conference reviewing crisis. Organized below by relevance bucket to the hypothesis.

> *Hypothesis recap*: as the number of papers in prestigious journals/conferences grows, the signaling effect of gate-keeping diminishes, making it harder to assess paper or researcher quality from acceptance.

## A. Foundational theory (most relevant for the model)

| File | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| `2312_10896_market_lemons_regulator_signaling.pdf` | Akerlof "Lemons" model with a third-party regulator (DMV) that issues quality certificates. Shows when costly certification breaks down → analog of our publication system. |
| `heckman_moktan_top_five_economics.pdf` | NBER WP 25093 (Heckman & Moktan, 2018). The empirical anchor: T5 econ journals function as career gatekeepers but are imperfect filters; many top-1% influential papers appear outside the T5. |
| `2603_00069_top_performers_top_journals_concentration.pdf` | Kwiek & Roszka (2026). 144K Polish scientists 1992–2021. Publishing in top-decile journals is a *persistent stratification mechanism*; explicitly frames top journals as "selection gates". |
| `2105_03166_bayesian_info_cascade.pdf` | Bayesian model of information cascades — relevant for modelling how acceptance signals propagate and lose information. |
| `1303_7274_reputation_impact_academic_careers.pdf` | Reputation dynamics in academic careers — connects acceptance signal to long-run career outcomes. |
